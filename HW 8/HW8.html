Homework 8: Geoprocessing Analysis

Fionn Kenny

This map intends to show the accessibility of New York City high schools through public transport. In order to do this, I decided to categorize high schools by if they were within a 10 minute walk, or half a mile, of a subway stop. In order to do this, first I imported a shapefile of Subway Lines and Subway Stops in New York City. Then, I imported a CSV of public high schools from the Department of Education, with Longitude and Latitude information. I created an 800 meter buffer around the high schools and took the intersection of schools within the buffers, shown in orange. We can see the high schools without subway access are largely in the outer boroughs, including far Queens, Brookyln, and all high schools in Staten Island, which lacks a subway.
